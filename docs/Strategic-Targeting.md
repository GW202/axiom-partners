## 4. Channel Strategy & Budget Allocation

### Channel Mix

| Channel | Budget Share | Role in Funnel | Primary Persona |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Google Search Ads** | 50% | Bottom-of-funnel — capture active intent | All personas (highest intent) |
| **LinkedIn Ads** | 30% | Top/mid-funnel — target decision-makers | Jennifer (Asset), David (Facility), Patricia (Property) |
| **Google Display/Retargeting** | 15% | Mid-funnel — re-engage website visitors | All personas (retargeting) |
| **Meta/Facebook** | 5% | Top-of-funnel — local business awareness | Patricia (Property), local business owners |

### Why This Split

**Google Search (50%)** captures people actively searching for commercial cleaning services. These are the highest-intent, lowest-cost leads. Axiom's 155+ landing pages give us a massive advantage — every ad can point to a hyper-relevant, city+service-specific page instead of a generic homepage. Industry benchmarks show cleaning companies achieve a 17.65% conversion rate on search ads — well above the industry average.

**LinkedIn (30%)** reaches decision-makers who aren't actively searching but match our ideal customer profile. A facility director managing a 200,000 sq ft corporate campus may not be Googling "commercial cleaning" today, but a targeted LinkedIn ad highlighting how Axiom reduced a similar client's vacancy rate from 12% to 4% plants a seed. LinkedIn also feeds the Google retargeting pool.

**Display/Retargeting (15%)** brings back the 82–97% of website visitors who don't convert on the first visit. Someone who visited `/orlando-office-cleaning` but didn't fill out the consultation form sees Axiom ads across the web for the next 30–90 days.

**Meta/Facebook (5%)** provides low-cost local awareness and retargeting. Many property managers and building owners are active on Facebook. Budget is intentionally small — this is a supplementary channel, not a primary driver.
